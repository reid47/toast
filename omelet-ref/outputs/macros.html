Macros

Omelet lets you define reusable blocks of template code, called macros, to encourage a more modular, DRY approach to building templates. Macros can take zero or more parameters and are dynamically-scoped (they are always evaluated in the context in which they are called, and have access to all of the variables in that context).

Macro definitions look like this:

Omelet template:
>def info(name,age):
    {name} is {age} years old.

>def headline(text):
    @h1.bigHeadline {text}

>def link_to(url,text):
    @a[href={url}] {text}

>def page_title():
    Welcome to my web page!

On their own, macro definitions do not contribute any output to the rendered HTML pages. If you want to use a macro, you can call it within an interpolation, as shown below. These interpolated macro calls do contribute output to the rendered HTML.

Assuming the macros above are still in scope, we can refer to them in these examples:

Omelet template:
{info("Reid",22)}
{headline("Hello, world!")}
{link_to("example.com","click here")}
{page_title()}
Rendered HTML:
Reid is 22 years old.
<h1 class="bigHeadline">Hello, world!<h1>
<a href="example.com">click here</a>
Welcome to my web page!
